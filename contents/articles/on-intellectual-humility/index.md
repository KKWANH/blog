# On Intellectual Humility

Humility is often misread as softness or indecision. In technical work it is closer to calibration. It is the practice of holding a model firmly enough to use it and lightly enough to replace it.

## Precision Requires Revision

The person who never revises rarely appears uncertain, but that is not the same as being accurate. A system improves because it admits mismatch between prediction and reality.

## Scale Punishes Overconfidence

The cost of false certainty rises with scale. Once a belief shapes architecture, team process, or editorial direction, its errors propagate outward. Humility limits blast radius.

## Practical Rule

The useful habit is simple: state assumptions clearly enough that they can later be disproved. Ambiguity protects the ego and weakens the work.
